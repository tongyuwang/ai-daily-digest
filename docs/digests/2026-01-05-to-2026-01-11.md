# AI Digest - Jan 5 to Jan 11, 2026

*Weekly roundup of AI bookmarks from X/Twitter*

---

## Key Themes

1. **Ralph Wiggum Takes Over** — The "Ralph Wiggum" technique for autonomous AI coding (ship features while you sleep) went absolutely viral this week, spawning tutorials, breakdowns, TUIs, and multi-model variants.
2. **Agent Evals & Quality** — Anthropic published their definitive guide on evaluating AI agents, sparking a wave of discussion about how to actually measure whether agents are doing good work.
3. **Claude Code Democratization** — Free courses, SDK tutorials, and non-coder resources proliferated, making Claude Code accessible to an entirely new audience.
4. **The Socratic Method for AI** — A fascinating counter-trend: the most productive agent users *ask* instead of *tell*, treating agents as thinking partners.
5. **Vibe Coding Principles** — The community began codifying best practices for working with AI coding agents into shareable frameworks.

## Synthesis

This was the week Ralph Wiggum went viral. The technique — writing detailed PRDs and letting AI agents ship features autonomously while you sleep — captured the imagination of the entire AI coding community. Matt Pocock's breakdown, Ryan Carson's system diagrams, Daisy Hollman's viral prompt, and even a dedicated TUI (ralph-tui.com) all emerged within days. But the really interesting counter-narrative came from Theodor Marcu and others pushing "Socratic mode" — arguing that the most productive users don't give agents instructions, they ask them questions. Meanwhile, Anthropic dropped their agent evals guide, Martin Fowler published fragments on how AI is changing internal dev practices, and the Agent SDK enabled a new wave of builders. The week also saw an explosion in educational content, with free courses from Carl Vellotti and Andrew Ng making agent-powered development accessible to non-coders.
